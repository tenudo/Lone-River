× 25th August

Clusters of wild chervil are blossoming at the edge of the woods. The indifferent wolves rush past, of course, but I stopped a while and watched them. From overhead the leaves patterned their petals with a trembling lattice of light and shadow.

It's not baby's breath, but it reminds me of that bouquet you gave me once; little stardust bursts of white clustered among the clover. And roses, of course, and lilies. I remember how they looked, pale and gleaming, lying in spades of glass against the cool blue-patterned tile. What a shame, I thought, but I should have known better. I was too young, back then.

× 23rd September

It was cold this morning. I saw Cress and Flax curled up together, breathing in tandem in a clutch of reeds. Cress's snout was tucked up under Flax's chin with agonizing tenderness; nearby a moth made a pass at a pair of cattails. I think that's when I started crying.

How can you hold my hand the way you do and not feel like you're dying?

× 8th October

The water is clear and still. I can see myself reflected with startling clarity; it's been a while since I've seen a person's face. Across the lake lies only open forest, deep blue pines and an empty sun.

The wolves are out today. Gone, I mean. Perhaps they, too, have run to lonelier forests, to solitude, to sorrow, from sorrow. So it goes.
